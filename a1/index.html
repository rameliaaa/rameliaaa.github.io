Responding to: luni.app

  • 1) What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience? When I first opened up luni, I found myself drawn to the soft pastel colours of the site that went along with the playful graphics that seemed to move when being interacted with. Further, the smooth nature of the scroll between pages intrigued me.
  • 2) Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions. Scrolled between pages, Clicked on clickable elements that made the graphics move, Continuing to play around with engaging elements and shift around the moving elements of the site. Briefly clicked on the linked information
  • 3) What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with? I definitely felt I spent the majority of my time engaging with the home pages especially over the information parts of each section. I found the visuals used within these home pages most eye catching and interesting.
  • 4) What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience? Moving between the different home pages that were displayed like little worlds, playing around with how little things moved when you hovered over them and clicked on them. Some were also game-like features where if you scored, your points would go up.
  • 5) What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience? I felt luni wanted to intrigue and showcase the creative nature of their company and careers to potential customers and clients. Specifically those who were interested in the site's aesthetics and engagement.
  • 6) How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal? Throughout the site, the company uses both a modern yet artsy aesthetic as well as playful user interaction. Their site is overall quite advanced with this user engagement therefore putting a good first impression on creatives interested in careers in their company.
  • 7) What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times) My impression of the reaction time of luni, is the first one or two times entering the site you begin to interact with the different movable worlds that display each homepage. Throughout this the longer spent the more you are able to find the little details of user experience with hidden movables. It's then after searching through these four pages you might click on the discoveries of each homepage taking a slower approach to scrolling through each section of information. I believe this aspect would only be engaged once unless returning to specific information.
  • 8) How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time? I feel luni’s design is created to discover more hidden elements the longer you interact with it. Little movements and expressions only really become noticeable to the user after revisiting pages, clicking on random items and moving your cursor in places not originally seen as containing information.
  • 9) What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference? Within each user interaction there is obvious reference to basic mobile games with singular purposes. However, the way the homepages are designed makes me think of more complex pc/console indie game aesthetics.
  • 10) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience? The playful game-like references make you want to mess around with luni’s site, engaging with every piece of information in a pursuit of a kind of gratification. This could reference the company's hands-on and ambitious traits and about trying to succeed highly with your goals.
  • 11) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience? I feel like luni wants you to feel ambitious and aspired by their site and its features, a theme of success is relevant throughout their game-like features of the homepages. To score goals within your career and hopefully score goals for them within a career at luni. I feel like they also evoke a sense of community and sustainability just following along with the indie aesthetic commonly associated with a smaller business.
  • 12) What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating? I felt the placement of the index to be a little bit inconvenient for users as it sometimes popped up when trying to click on other aspects such as the link to the discover page or even when engaging with the movable objects in the homepage. I also wasn’t interested in the aesthetic choices made to the index, it's programmed to lean slightly depending on where you move your cursor and whilst I get the aesthetic relevance, I just found it a bit annoying.
  • 13) What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying? The aesthetic choices such as the colour and texture of the page and its overall cohesiveness was very satisfying. Further, The smoothness of the scroll and click movements were very enjoyable.
